## Rules

1. **All resources are permitted** — AI tools (including Claude Code), the internet, and talking with other candidates. Use whatever helps.
2. **In the final presentation, you must be able to independently explain your design decisions** — regardless of what help you used along the way, you need to own and articulate your choices.
3. **Business context questions** may be directed to Kean; technical questions should be handled on your own or with AI.
4. **Checkpoint submission times are fixed**; late or missing submissions will be noted.
5. **The final presentation is conducted by the reviewer**, 10 minutes per person — come prepared to demo and explain your work.

## Checkpoint Details

### Checkpoint 1 (10:30): Technical Design Document

Submit a brief written design — any format is fine. Suggested content:

* The MVP scope you plan to implement
* Your data structure approach
* How you plan to integrate AI

**No penalty for not submitting, but quality is factored into the evaluation.**

\---

### Checkpoint 2 (13:00): Progress Update

A 3–5 sentence description of where you are is sufficient.
After submitting, you will receive a **requirement change notice** — please continue development accordingly.

\---

### Checkpoint 3 (15:00): Final Submission

Please submit both of the following:

1. Your code (Git URL or code directory)
2. A short written note covering:

   * Key design decisions and why you made them
   * Known limitations or issues
   * What you would change if you had more time

\---

## Final Presentation (20 minutes)

You have 20 minutes — use them however you'd like. Suggested structure:

* Live demo (show your work running)
* Walk through your key design decisions
* The reviewer will ask questions during this time

**Tip**: Decide in advance on the 2–3 points you most want to convey — you don't need to cover everything.
